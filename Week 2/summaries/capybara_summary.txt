The Wikipedia page on Capybaras provides detailed information about this large rodent species, known scientifically as Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris. Capybaras are native to South America and are the largest rodents in the world, typically inhabiting savannas, forests, and areas near water bodies. The page covers various aspects of capybara biology, including their classification, physical description, ecology, social behavior, reproduction, and interactions with humans. Capybaras are social animals that often live in groups, primarily feed on grasses and aquatic plants, and have adapted to various environments, including urban settings. They are not considered endangered and have stable populations across their natural range. The article also discusses their cultural significance and mentions how capybaras have become popular in media and as social media icons in recent years.
